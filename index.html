User Guide & Help

What is nodrift?

nodrift is a self-contained browser app for logging and analyzing your daily work sessions. All data is stored locally in your browser — no account or internet connection required after the page loads.

Resilient Dual-Storage Engine

To protect your database from browser crashes or sudden tab closures, the app uses a dual-layer storage layout. Logs are written asynchronously to a local IndexedDB database, and simultaneously mirrored to a synchronous Emergency Sync Backup in local storage. On load, the app automatically checks and restores any missing records.

Interface at a Glance

  • Left Panel — Live Tracker: Your active session controls, live timers, and daily progress bar.
  • Right Panel — Insights / Logbook: Switch between your weekly analytics view and your full shift history using the two tabs at the top of the panel.
  • Footer Toolbar: Access CSV export, clipboard copy, JSON backup/restore, leave management, help, settings, and the factory reset (Wipe).
  • Header Bar: Set your active timezone, configure your daily goal, and switch the app theme.

Quick Start (3 Steps)

  • Step 1 — Verify your goal: Before starting, review your Daily Goal in the header. It dynamically auto-populates to keep you on pace for a 40-hour week (or 6 hours on weekends). You can click the field to open presets, or type a custom value. The goal for each day is permanently locked the moment you submit your first shift, so set it correctly upfront.
  • Step 2 — Track your day: Click Start Work when you begin. Use Take Break when stepping away and Resume Work to continue. The Production and Break timers update in real time.
  • Step 3 — Submit your shift: Click Submit EOD at the end of your day. A dialog will prompt you to add shift notes before the entry is written to your Logbook permanently.

Keyboard Shortcuts Reference

The application features a rich suite of keyboard hotkeys designed specifically for power users. All active mappings are listed below:

  • Spacebar: Starts a new shift, takes a break, or resumes active work.
  • S: Opens the End of Day (EOD) submission modal.
  • Ctrl + S / Cmd + S: Downloads the backup JSON file and opens an email draft template.
  • Alt + 1 / Alt + 2: Swaps directly between the Insights and Logbook tabs.
  • Question Mark (?): Opens this Help Guide overlay.
  • Escape: Exits input fields, closes modal overlays, collapses the filter drawer, and deselects highlighted logs.
  • Tab: Navigates fields sequentially with focus traps active inside modals.
  • Alt + K: Opens the Logbook search box and expands the advanced filters automatically.
  • Alt + N: Launches the Add Manual Log modal instantly.
  • Alt + T: Cycles through the four design themes (Vercel Dark, Cobalt Glass, etc.).
  • Alt + Plus/Minus (= / -): Fine-tunes your Daily Goal by +/- 15 mins.
  • Alt + Left / Alt + Right: Steps back and forward through the Insights calendar week.
  • H: Jumps the Insights timeline instantly back to the current week.
  • Selected Log Actions: Highlight an entry with the arrow keys, then press:
    • E — Edit the selected shift record.
    • C — Copy the shift record cleanly to your clipboard.
    • N or Enter — View shift notes in a popup overlay.
    • D or Backspace / Delete — Trigger the deletion confirmation flow.

Activating Keyboard Navigation

To use the log-specific keyboard actions (Edit, Copy, View Notes, Delete), you must first activate keyboard focus on the log list. You can trigger this by pressing Alt + K to focus the filter search box, or by pressing Tab sequentially until focus lands on the log rows. Use the Up / Down Arrow Keys to highlight individual log cards, and press the corresponding action keys.

Session Controls

  • Start Work: Begins a new session and starts the Production timer. Only available when no session is active.
  • Take Break: Pauses the Production timer and starts the Break timer. Available while working.
  • Resume Work: Stops the Break timer and resumes Production. Replaces the Take Break button while on break.
  • Submit EOD: Ends and saves the current session to your Logbook. You will be prompted to enter shift notes. This action is final — the shift is written to permanent storage.
  • Adjust Timer: Click the adjustment icon in the footer to open the floating Adjust Time popup.
    • Context-Aware Safety: Automatically targets whichever mode is currently active (Work or Break) and disables input controls when no session is running.
    • Smart Offset Parsing: Supports decimal hours (e.g., 1.5h), composite offsets (e.g., 1h 15m 30s), and bare digits (which default to minutes).
    • Real-Time Preview: Above the popup, a floating tooltip displays the projected time calculation as you type (color-coded to match the target timer) and alerts you with a warning if absolute clock notation (such as : or AM/PM) is used.
    • Deficit Protection: If you subtract more time than has currently accumulated, the app prevents negative numbers by capping the timer at zero and advancing your initial shift start time (login timestamp) forward to absorb the difference.
    • Instant Undo: After applying an adjustment, a notification toast appears with an Undo button, letting you instantly roll back and recover your original timer states.

Progress Bar & ETA

  • The horizontal bar between the two timers shows your progress toward today's daily goal.
  • Left label: Current completion percentage and remaining time.
  • Right label: Estimated end-of-day time in BST based on your current pace.
  • The bar turns fully green once your goal is reached.

Dynamic Daily Goal Auto-Population

To help you stay on track for your 40-hour weekly target, the daily goal automatically populates with your current pacing requirement each time a new session starts:

  • Weekdays (Mon–Fri): The goal calculates dynamically based on the cumulative hours needed to reach the 40-hour weekly target [5]. For example, if you log 10 hours on Monday, Tuesday's goal auto-populates to 6 hours [5]. Conversely, working 7 hours on Monday auto-populates Tuesday's goal to 9 hours [5]. Scheduled leaves on prior weekdays automatically deduct 8 hours from your cumulative weekly target [5].
  • Weekends (Sat–Sun): The weekend daily goal defaults to a fixed 6 hours.
  • Manual Adjustments & Custom Presets: The daily goal is only an initial recommendation. You can override it at any point using presets, the −15m / +15m adjusters, or by typing directly in the header. You can also double-click any preset pill to permanently assign your own custom duration to it.

Goal Anchor & Split Shifts

When you submit your first shift on any given day, the daily goal active at that moment is permanently saved ("anchored") to that entry [5]. This ensures that changing tomorrow's goal does not retroactively alter your historical records [5].

Intra-Day Deficit Auto-Adjustment (Split Shifts): If there is already a logged shift for today and you start a subsequent session, the header Daily Goal input automatically scales down to represent only the remaining deficit against today's anchored target.

Best practice: Always verify your Daily Goal in the header before submitting your first shift of the day [5]. If you need to correct a previously anchored goal, go to the Logbook tab, click the Edit icon on that entry, and update the goal value directly.

Intelligent Mode — Smart Tracking

When Intelligent mode is active (configured in Settings), the app runs three protective behaviors automatically:

  • Idle Lock: If your keyboard and mouse are inactive beyond the configured threshold (default: 60 minutes), the session pauses and a dialog appears when you return. You choose whether the idle period counts as work, break, or is discarded entirely. When returning from system sleep or tab suspension, a customized, distinct "System Pause Detected" state is presented to reassure you.
  • Sleep / Tab Suspension Detection: If your computer sleeps, the browser suspends the tab, or the system freezes, the app detects the gap on wake and prompts you to decide how to handle the missing time — the same as idle recovery.
  • Midnight Rollover: If a session is still active at 12:00 AM, the app automatically submits the current shift for the prior day and starts a clean session for the new date. No data is lost.

Simple mode disables the automatic idle and sleep suspension popups. However, Midnight Rollover is a universal safeguard that still runs in Simple mode if a timer is active, ensuring your shift times are cleanly partitioned across daily bounds without data loss.

Data Integrity Protections

  • Multiple Tab Prevention: The application actively prevents multiple tabs or instances from running simultaneously to protect your database from corruption.
  • Ghost Shift Detection: If you accidentally log a shift that is under 60 seconds, the UI will intercept it and throw a warning to prevent database spam.

Smart Time & Clock Inputs

Whenever you edit a duration or a clock time value (like in the Add Log or Edit Shift menus), you can use shorthand formats to speed up entry. The app will automatically format it when you press Enter or click away.

Work, Break, and Goal Durations:

  • Numbers: Typing 8 converts to 08:00:00 (or 08:00 for goals).
  • Decimals: Typing 8.5 converts to 08:30:00.
  • Minutes: Typing 45m converts to 00:45:00.
  • Hours & Minutes: Typing 8h 30m converts to 08:30:00.

Login and Logout Clock Times:

  • Raw Numbers: Typing 0900 or 900 auto-expands to 09:00:00 AM.
  • Suffix Shortcuts: Typing 900p or 900pm auto-expands to 09:00:00 PM.
  • Partial Colons: Typing 9:30 or 9:30p auto-resolves to AM/PM and pads seconds.
  • Auto-Meridian Fallback: If no AM/PM is typed, hours $\ge 12$ default to PM, and hours $< 12$ default to AM.

Storage Block Warnings

nodrift constantly validates browser storage health. If browser data writing fails (e.g., due to strict private browsing restrictions, disabled cookies, or running out of local disk space), a red alert badge will flash in the status bar: "RAM Only". If you see this, your data is only in temporary memory—download a JSON backup immediately using the toolbar to avoid losing your shift history.

Weekly Progress Overview

The Insights tab (right panel) shows your aggregated weekly performance. Use the left/right arrows to navigate between weeks. Double-click the week label to jump back to the current week.

  • Total Work / Total Break: Cumulative hours for the displayed week.
  • Weekly completion %: Your progress toward the 40-hour weekly target, shown in blue.
  • Status message: A short contextual summary — e.g., whether you are on track, ahead, or behind pace for the week.

Consistency Heatmap

Click the Heatmap icon in the header or toolbar to open the Consistency Heatmap modal. This visual calendar provides a macroscopic view of your tracking consistency and work density over time.

Advanced Interactive Features:

  • Real-Time Today Integration: Hovering over "today" on the heatmap dynamically merges your active running session's work and break times on the fly, rendering a live-updating preview.
  • Click-to-Filter Navigation: Clicking any cell in the heatmap instantly closes the modal, navigates the Insights base date to that target week, switches your active panel tab to Logbook, automatically expands the Advanced Filters drawer, and injects that date directly into the search filter to show you that specific day's records.
  • YTD Annual Aggregates: Displays cumulative annual stats (YTD Work Hours and total Leave days) at the bottom left of the modal.

M–T–W–T–F Day Segments

The five horizontal bars represent Monday through Friday of the selected week. Each bar fills proportionally based on hours logged against that day's goal.

Interactive Segment Hover Tooltips: Hovering over any Monday-Friday progress segment bar in the Insights tab reveals a high-density popup detailing date, day, work, break, variance (with O/U badges), progress %, goal, and a list of all notes logged for that day.

  • A bar that is fully green means the daily goal was met or exceeded for that day.
  • A bar with a striped pattern indicates a scheduled leave day. Leave days are excluded from pace and goal calculations.
  • The current day's bar updates live as your session progresses.

Shift Badges

Each log entry in the weekly breakdown carries colored badges summarizing that shift:

  • W Work Time — Total productive hours logged.
  • B Break Time — Total break duration.
  • O Overtime — Shift met or exceeded its anchored goal.
  • U Under Goal — Shift did not reach its anchored goal.
  • G Goal Status — Visual indicator of daily goal attainment.

Pace Required

Displayed in the weekly breakdown, this figure tells you how many hours per day you need to average across the remaining active (non-leave) weekdays to reach your 40-hour weekly target. It recalculates automatically as you log hours each day.

Worked Leave Badge: If you work on a scheduled leave day, your hours are compiled but your pacing is protected. This is displayed with a specialized "Worked Leave" badge.

Note on Scheduled Leave: Scheduling a leave day automatically deducts exactly 8 hours from your cumulative weekly 40-hour pacing target, and that date is marked with a diagonal stripe pattern. This ensures your required daily pace recalculates fairly without penalizing your consistency scores.

Viewing & Navigating Entries

The Logbook tab (right panel) lists every submitted shift entry in reverse chronological order. Each entry shows the date, login/logout times, work and break duration, daily goal percentage, and any shift notes.

UI Interaction Shortcuts:

  • Click Row to Edit: Clicking anywhere on a log row (outside of action buttons) instantly launches the Edit Modal for that entry.
  • Scroll-to-Top Button: A floating scroll-to-top button automatically reveals itself when scrolling down the logbook past 150px, letting you jump back up easily.
  • Custom Picker Interfaces: Fully styled custom dropdowns and the Custom Date Picker calendar engine provide responsive year/month pagination, "Today" selection, and input clearing.

Adding a Manual Entry

Click Add Log in the Logbook header to open the manual entry form.

Dynamic Field Auto-Calculations: In both Add Manual Shift and Edit Shift modals, typing any two values of Login, Logout, and Work automatically calculates the third (tracking overrides cleanly). Changing Break dynamically updates either Work or Logout. If your Break time exceeds the total elapsed shift time, the field turns red and displays a warning block.

Deep Date Input Shorthands: You can type highly rapid shorthands into any date field and press Enter or blur to auto-expand them:

  • t / today / 0 → today's date.
  • y / yesterday / -1 → yesterday's date.
  • Negative Integers: typing -5 calculates and outputs the date of exactly 5 days ago.
  • Natural Monthly Strings: expressions like 15th jan or jan 15 auto-map to the correct date.
  • Smart Digits Auto-Expansion: 8 digits (12252026) and 6 digits (122526) expand to 12/25/26. 4 digits (1225) auto-fills the current year. Typing 1 or 2 digits (e.g., 15) auto-completes to day 15 of the current month and year.

Editing & Deleting Entries

  • Edit (pencil icon): Opens the entry in an editable form. You can update any field, including the anchored goal, notes, hours, or date. Save to commit changes.
  • Delete (trash icon): Permanently removes the entry. This action cannot be undone. A confirmation prompt will appear before deletion.

Filters & Saved Views

Once you have configured a filter combination you use regularly, save it as a named Saved View. Saved views appear in the dropdown at the top of the filter drawer and can be recalled with a single click. You can also delete custom views by selecting them and clicking the delete option inside the filter drawer.

Managing Leaves & Holidays

Click the Leaves icon in the footer toolbar to open the leave manager. Add scheduled leave days (annual leave, sick days, public holidays) by date. Leave days are rendered with a striped pattern in the Insights day segments and are excluded from pace and weekly goal calculations.

Export & Sharing

  • CSV (spreadsheet icon): Downloads a .csv file of all visible log entries. Columns include date, login/logout times, work hours (decimal), break hours, daily goal, and notes. Suitable for submitting to HR or importing into Excel / Google Sheets.
  • Copy (clipboard icon): Copies your log history to the clipboard as a formatted plain-text email summary. Entries are grouped by day, work/break totals are shown per day, and any shift under 7 hours is flagged. Paste directly into a Gmail draft with Ctrl+V / Cmd+V.

Backup & Restore

  • Save / JSON backup (download icon): Downloads a .json file containing your entire log database and app state. Store this file somewhere safe. All data lives in your browser's local storage — clearing browser data or switching devices will erase it without a backup.
  • Load / JSON restore (upload icon): Imports a previously saved .json backup file. Existing logs are merged with the imported data. If there are duplicates or colliding entries, an interactive Import Conflict Wizard will guide you through resolving them.
  • Recommended cadence: Export a JSON backup at least once per week.

Settings ( icon)

  • Simple / Intelligent mode: Switch between tracking modes. Intelligent mode enables idle lock, sleep detection, and midnight rollover. Simple mode runs timers continuously without any interruption or auto-detection.
  • Enable Idle Lock: Toggle automatic idle detection on or off (Intelligent mode only).
  • Idle Threshold: The slider sets how long you must be inactive (5–120 minutes) before the idle lock triggers. Default is 60 minutes.
  • Auto-Populate Daily Goal: Toggle whether the app automatically adjusts your daily goal to keep you on pace for the 40-hour weekly target.
  • Auto-save Backup: When enabled, the app automatically downloads a JSON backup file at the end of each day when you submit your EOD shift.
  • Auto-email Summary: When enabled, submitting EOD automatically copies your log summary to the clipboard and opens a new Gmail draft, ready to paste.

Theme & Timezone

  • Theme: Click the theme toggle button in the header to open the theme picker. Choose from SF Dark, SF Light, Vercel Dark, or E-Ink. Your selection is saved automatically.
  • Timezone: Click the timezone label (PST/MST/CST/EST) in the header status bar to switch the primary clock timezone. The secondary clock always displays BST as a fixed reference. Your selection persists across sessions.
  • Daily Goal Presets: Click the Daily Goal field to open the preset picker. Use −15m / +15m to fine-tune, or double-click either preset pill to permanently reassign it to a custom value.

Database Safety & Recovery Systems

  • Restore Snapshot: Before any database write, nodrift automatically takes a backup state snapshot in local storage. If you ever make a mistake or lose tracking, open Settings () and select a prior timestamp from the Restore Snapshot list to instantly restore that exact database state.
  • Import Collision Conflict Wizard: When loading a .json backup, nodrift scans for overlapping dates. If conflicts occur, an interactive wizard will display a side-by-side comparison of local vs. backup records and prompt you to choose:
    • Keep Current Local: Discards the backup's overlapping shifts and keeps your local data intact.
    • Overwrite with Backup: Erases the local conflicting entries and writes the backup's records.
    • Keep Both: Imports all entries, creating duplicate shifts on those dates for you to resolve manually.
  • Multi-Tab Eviction Protocol: Opening nodrift in multiple browser tabs simultaneously triggers the Multiple Tabs Detected screen. To prevent clock conflicts and data corruption, only one tab can own the master tracking lease. Click Take Over Here on your desired tab to safely evict other instances, synchronize existing states, and resume tracking.

Factory Reset

The Wipe button (red trash icon) permanently deletes all logs, saved views, leave entries, and app settings. This action is irreversible. Always export a JSON backup before using this option.
